Guide

Welcome to Gupshup Campaign Manager. This guide will provide you step-by-step instructions and helpful tips to streamline workflows and unlock the full potential of conversational campaigns

Campaign Manager Overview

Engage, nudge and convert customers with personalized marketing campaigns on WhatsApp

Creating a Template

Templates is the first step towards creating a campaign. WhatsApp categorizes messages under three template types:

  1. Utility Messages: Messages that are transactional in nature, e.g. post-purchase notifications, billing statements
  2. Marketing Messages: Messages that are promotional in nature and do not fall under the Utility category. Choose from a range of marketing message types: Custom message, Catalogue message, Multi-Product message, Carousels, Limited Time Offer and etc. To know more about the marketing message types, click on the 'Explore Templates' button below
  3. Authentication Messages: Messages relating to authenticating users with OTPs

Steps to create a template

Templates is the first step towards creating a campaign. WhatsApp categorizes messages under three template types:

  1. Click on “Templates” on the left panel, navigate to > Create template.
  2. Fill the template details and submit for approval. Once submitted, it might take a minute for the status to reflect.

Tips:

  • Be clear about the message purpose and select the right category for hassle-free approval
  • To avoid rejections due to incorrect category, tick “Allow Meta to change category while approving
  • DO NOT copy and submit an existing template as-is without deleting the old one, as it can be rejected with error 'Invalid category' due to duplication

Creating a Broadcast Campaign

  1. From 'Campaigns' on the left panel navigate to "Broadcast" -> New Broadcast
  2. Enter the campaign Name and select the WABA number you want to send the campaign from
  3. Upload the contacts list. If your plan supports Customer360* then instead of uploading a list, you can also create a segment and personalize your campaigns based on customer attributes (such as gender, location, age) or events (such as order placed, abandoned cart, viewed message etc.)
  4. Check the link tracking box if you want to capture engagement with links. Only links inserted through variables in a template message or 'Dynamic link tracking' CTA button can be tracked. (Note: Links added directly in the campaign message cannot be tracked. To know more about link tracking, click on the "Learn more" button below)
  5. Select the Ad ID, if you are planning to send the broadcast as a retargeting campaign for leads generated from a Click to WhatsApp Ad
  6. Choose an approved template, upload media, add variable values and fallback
  7. Preview the message and proceed to send the broadcast campaign, or schedule for later
  8. If your template encourages customer response, click on “Make it interactive” on the preview page and create a journey for the campaign. Watch a demo of interactive campaigns using the button below.

Analyzing a Broadcast Campaign Performance

Now that you've sent the campaign, dig into the analytics and define your retargeting and optimization tactics

  1. To access analytics, click on the 'Campaign Title' in the campaign list
  2. View metrics such as - messages delivered, read, failed
  3. Go macro with metrics such as total clicks, unique clicks and CTR of links in the campaign

For detailed customer wise metrics such as IP address, device, OS and time stamps of message delivery, views and clicks, download the Response file.
